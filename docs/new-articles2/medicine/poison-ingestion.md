In a survival scenario, poisoning risk increases dramatically — unfamiliar plants, improvised chemicals, contaminated water, stored fuel and chemicals in salvaged buildings, and medication errors. The general rule: when in doubt, treat it as a poisoning emergency.

## First Response

1. Remove the person from the source. If chemical fumes, get to fresh air.
2. If on skin, remove contaminated clothing and flush skin with water for 15-20 minutes.
3. If in eyes, flush with water for 20 minutes.
4. If swallowed, identify what was ingested and approximately how much.
5. Do NOT induce vomiting unless specifically indicated — many poisons cause more damage coming back up (caustics, petroleum products).
6. Do NOT give milk — this is a myth for most poisonings and can speed absorption.

## Activated Charcoal

The single most important poisoning treatment available without a hospital. Activated charcoal binds to most poisons in the stomach and prevents absorption.

Dose: 1 g/kg body weight. For a 70 kg adult, that's 70 grams — roughly a full standard bottle of activated charcoal suspension. For children: 1 g/kg.

Timing: most effective within 1 hour of ingestion. Some benefit up to 2 hours. After 4 hours, minimal value.

Mix with water if you have powder/capsules. It tastes terrible — mix with cola or juice if available.

Does NOT work for: alcohol, iron, lithium, potassium, strong acids/alkalis, petroleum products.

> Stock activated charcoal. It's cheap, shelf-stable for years, and there is no substitute in the field.

## Common Survival-Scenario Poisonings

**Mushroom poisoning:** Symptoms within 6 hours (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea) are usually less dangerous species. Symptoms delayed 6-24 hours suggest Amatoxin poisoning (death cap/destroying angel) — this is a liver failure emergency with high mortality. Activated charcoal, aggressive hydration, and evacuation if any option exists.

**Plant poisoning:** See the poisonous plants article. Water hemlock, poison hemlock, and death camas are the most commonly fatal. Symptoms: nausea, vomiting, seizures, cardiac arrhythmia depending on species.

**Antifreeze (ethylene glycol):** Sweet-tasting, attractive to children. Causes kidney failure. If caught early, the antidote is ethanol (hard liquor) — it competes for the same enzyme and slows metabolism of the poison. Give vodka or whiskey: loading dose of roughly 1 ml/kg of 40% alcohol, then 0.15 ml/kg/hour.

**Carbon monoxide:** From fires, generators, or engines in enclosed spaces. Headache, dizziness, confusion, cherry-red skin (late sign). Move to fresh air immediately. Give high-flow oxygen if available. Unconscious patients need CPR.

**Medication overdose:** Acetaminophen overdose is the most common and most dangerous — liver failure develops over 24-72 hours and may not show symptoms until it's too late. If more than 150 mg/kg is ingested (roughly 15 extra-strength tablets for an adult), give activated charcoal immediately.

## When to Induce Vomiting

Almost never. The old advice to keep ipecac syrup is outdated. Vomiting is only appropriate for:
- A known highly toxic plant ingestion within the last 30 minutes
- When activated charcoal is unavailable
- When the substance is NOT caustic (acid/alkali) or petroleum-based

Method: 1 tablespoon of salt in a glass of warm water, drink quickly. Or stimulate the gag reflex. This is a last resort.
