Situations may arise where you need to move through an area while avoiding detection — hostile groups, civil unrest, or areas under someone else's control. The principles are simple: be unseen, be unheard, leave no trace.

## Counter-Tracking

If someone is tracking you, your footprints are the biggest giveaway.

1. Walk on hard surfaces — rock, packed trails, roads, gravel. Avoid soft ground, mud, and fresh snow.
2. Walk in water when possible. Enter and exit streams on rock.
3. Step on rocks and roots, not between them.
4. Brush out tracks behind you in soft ground with a leafy branch — but don't overdo it. Swept ground is obvious to a trained tracker.
5. Walk through areas with heavy leaf litter or pine needles — these spring back and hide footprints.
6. Change direction at a hard surface. Walk 50 feet on a road, then exit on the opposite side from where you entered.
7. Travel during rain — it erases tracks quickly.

## Movement Patterns

Do NOT move in a straight line between your origin and destination — that's predictable. Use offset routes: approach your destination from an unexpected direction. Zig-zag through terrain. Use terrain features (ridges, gullies, tree lines) as cover.

Move during low-visibility periods: dawn, dusk, rain, fog, heavy cloud cover. Full darkness is safest but slowest. Avoid silhouetting yourself on ridgelines.

Travel alone or in pairs. Large groups are noisy and leave obvious trails.

## Hiding

If you need to stop and hide:

1. Find a position with cover (stops observation) AND concealment (stops bullets/physical access). A bush provides concealment. A rock wall provides cover.
2. Get off trails and away from obvious hiding spots — these are searched first.
3. Stay low. Most people scan at eye level.
4. Control your outline, shadow, shine, smell, and sound. No reflective surfaces, no bright colors, no fire, no cooking smells, minimal talking.
5. If dogs are tracking you, cross water. Move through areas with strong smells (diesel, fertilizer, pine sap). Change elevation frequently — scent rises.

## If Captured

Stay calm. Do not resist violently unless you assess an immediate threat to your life. Give minimal information. Observe everything — guard rotations, routes, keys, weaknesses, terrain outside. Plan your escape before attempting it — a failed escape attempt usually results in tighter security or worse treatment.

The best time to escape is immediately — before you're moved to a more secure location, before your captors are organized, and while you're still closest to your starting point.
