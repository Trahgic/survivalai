Sourdough is bread leavened with wild yeast captured from the air — no commercial yeast needed. A living sourdough starter can be maintained indefinitely, passed down through generations. In a survival context, it turns flour and water into real bread without any manufactured ingredients.

## Creating a Starter From Scratch

1. Day 1: Mix 1/2 cup flour and 1/4 cup lukewarm water in a clean jar. Stir well. Cover loosely (cloth or loose lid — needs air exchange). Leave at room temperature.
2. Day 2: You may see small bubbles or nothing at all. Add another 1/2 cup flour and 1/4 cup water. Stir. Cover.
3. Days 3-5: The mixture should start bubbling and developing a sour smell. Each day, discard half the starter and add 1/2 cup flour and 1/4 cup water. This "feeding" provides fresh food for the yeast.
4. Days 5-7: The starter should be reliably doubling in size within 4-8 hours after feeding. It smells pleasantly sour, like yogurt or beer. It's ready to bake with.

Whole wheat or rye flour starts faster than white flour because the bran carries more wild yeast. Once established, you can feed with any flour.

## Maintaining the Starter

Feed once daily if kept at room temperature. Or refrigerate and feed once a week. To feed: discard half, add equal parts flour and water by weight (or roughly equal by volume). Stir vigorously.

A healthy starter doubles in 4-8 hours after feeding, smells pleasantly sour, and has a web of bubbles throughout. If it smells like acetone or nail polish remover, it's hungry — feed it twice a day for a few days.

## Basic Sourdough Bread

1. Mix 1 cup active starter, 3 cups flour, 1 cup water, 1.5 teaspoons salt.
2. Knead 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Add flour if too sticky.
3. Cover and let rise 4-12 hours (sourdough is slower than commercial yeast — this is normal). It should roughly double.
4. Shape into a round or oblong loaf. Place on a floured surface or in a floured bowl, seam-side up.
5. Let rise again 2-4 hours until puffy.
6. Slash the top with a sharp knife (allows expansion).
7. Bake at 450°F for 30-40 minutes. The crust should be dark brown and the loaf should sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Without an oven: bake in a covered Dutch oven in coals, in a clay oven, or as flatbread in a hot pan.

## Why Sourdough Matters for Survival

Beyond morale (fresh bread is transformative for group morale), sourdough fermentation breaks down phytic acid in grains, making minerals more bioavailable. It also partially pre-digests gluten, making the bread easier to digest. The lactic acid bacteria produce natural preservatives — sourdough bread lasts 5-7 days before molding, compared to 2-3 days for regular bread.
