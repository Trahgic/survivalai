A rope bridge gets people and gear across a gap that's too wide to jump and too dangerous to wade. They range from a simple single-rope crossing to a full three-rope bridge walkable with gear.

## Single Rope Bridge (Commando Crossing)

Minimum equipment: one strong rope, long enough to span the gap plus 20 feet for anchoring on each side.

1. The strongest swimmer or most confident person crosses first — by swimming, wading, or fording — carrying the end of the rope.
2. Anchor both ends to solid trees, rocks, or deadman anchors (a log buried horizontally in a trench). The rope must be anchored at the SAME HEIGHT on both sides or slightly higher on the far side.
3. Tension the rope using a trucker's hitch or mechanical advantage system (Z-rig: run the rope through a carabiner on the far anchor, back through a carabiner on the near side, pull). The rope should be taut but will sag under load — that's normal.
4. Crossing: hang below the rope using hands and hooked ankles (monkey crawl), or clip in with a carabiner or sling and pull yourself across hand-over-hand.
5. Gear crosses separately: clip packs to the rope on a sling and pull across with a tag line.

## Three-Rope Bridge (Walkable)

Much more stable. Requires three ropes of equal length.

1. Bottom rope: foot rope. Tensioned tightly at waist height from the near bank.
2. Upper two ropes: hand ropes. Anchored at shoulder height, spaced 3-4 feet apart, tensioned.
3. Connect hand ropes to foot rope every 4-6 feet with vertical lashing (short rope ties connecting the hand rails to the foot rope). This creates a V-shape cross section.
4. Walk on the foot rope while holding the hand ropes. Lean slightly back into the hand ropes for balance.

## Anchoring

The anchor must hold the full weight of the rope plus the heaviest person crossing plus dynamic forces (bouncing). Minimum anchor: a tree at least 8 inches in diameter, or a buried deadman log.

**Deadman anchor:** dig a trench 3 feet deep perpendicular to the rope direction. Bury a log (at least 4 inches diameter, 3 feet long) in the trench. Pass the rope around the log and backfill. Pack soil tightly. This anchor holds thousands of pounds.

**Wrap anchoring on a tree:** wrap the rope around the tree 3 times, then tie off with two half-hitches. The friction of 3 wraps holds enormous load.

## Rope Selection

Minimum: 1/2 inch diameter static rope (climbing rope, utility rope). Dynamic (stretchy) rope works but sags more. Paracord (550 cord) is NOT strong enough for a bridge — it's rated at 550 lbs, and a bridge needs to hold 1,000+ lbs when accounting for dynamic loading.

For improvised rope bridges over short gaps (under 20 feet), two or three lengths of paracord braided together can work for lightweight crossers.

## Safety

- Only one person on the bridge at a time.
- Clip gear to a safety line attached to the bridge rope.
- Have a downstream safety person with a throw rope in case someone falls.
- Test the bridge with a heavy pack before sending a person.
- Bridges over water: the midpoint will sag. In fast water, even a slight sag means the midpoint is in the current. Tension harder or accept getting wet at the middle.
