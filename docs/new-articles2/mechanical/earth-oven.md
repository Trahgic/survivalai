An earth oven is a dome of clay and sand that retains heat for hours after a fire is built inside. You fire it up, scrape out the coals, and bake bread, pizza, roasts, and casseroles in the stored radiant heat. They've been built on every continent for thousands of years.

## Quick-Build Cob Oven (One Day)

1. **Foundation:** Build a platform of stone, brick, or cinder blocks about waist height and 4-5 feet square. This gets the oven to a comfortable working height and keeps it off wet ground.

2. **Floor:** Lay firebricks (or flat dense stones) on the platform in a circle about 30 inches in diameter. These are your baking surface. Level them carefully with sand underneath.

3. **Sand form:** Build a dome of damp sand on the brick floor. This is your mold. Dome should be about 16 inches tall at center. Cover the sand dome with wet newspaper — this makes it easy to remove later.

4. **Thermal layer:** Mix cob — 1 part clay soil, 3 parts coarse sand, a handful of straw chopped short. Add water until it holds together. Apply over the sand form in a 3-4 inch thick layer. Build from the bottom up, pressing firmly. Leave a door opening about 10 inches wide and 10 inches tall (roughly 63% of the interior dome height — this ratio is important for proper draft).

5. **Let dry 24 hours.** Do NOT light a fire yet.

6. **Dig out sand:** Reach through the door and scrape out all the sand. The newspaper separates easily from the clay.

7. **First fire:** Build a small fire inside. Increase fire size gradually over several hours. Large fires too soon crack the dome. Cure with progressively larger fires over 3-5 days.

8. **Insulation layer (optional but highly recommended):** After the thermal layer has dried and been cured with fire, add a 4-6 inch layer of insulating material — vermiculite mixed with clay slip, straw/clay mix, or sawdust/clay. This keeps heat in the thermal mass instead of radiating outward.

## Using the Oven

1. Build a fire inside the oven. Burn for 2-3 hours until the interior walls have turned from black (soot) to white/gray (clean). The dome has absorbed enough heat.
2. Rake out all coals and ash. Sweep the floor with a damp rag on a stick.
3. Test temperature: throw a handful of flour on the floor. If it turns brown in 15 seconds, it's about 500°F (good for pizza and flatbread). If it takes 30 seconds, about 400°F (good for bread). If a minute, about 300°F (good for roasts and slow cooking).
4. Load food. Close the door (a wooden door won't burn against the oven face — it never gets hot enough on the outside).
5. Bread bakes in 30-45 minutes. Pizza in 3-5 minutes. A roast takes 2-4 hours.
6. The oven holds usable heat for 6-12 hours. Bake bread first (highest heat), then roasts, then slow dishes, then use the remaining heat to dry herbs or fruit.

## Dimensions That Matter

The door height should be 63% of the interior dome height. Too tall = heat escapes too fast. Too short = poor draft.

Minimum interior diameter for bread baking: 24 inches. A 30-inch interior fits 2-3 loaves comfortably. A 36-inch oven is excellent for a community/family.

## Maintenance

Cracks are normal and inevitable. Fill with clay slip (thin clay-water mixture pushed into cracks). The oven gets stronger with use as repeated heating cycles harden the clay further. Protect from rain with a roof overhang or removable cover. A well-maintained earth oven lasts for years.
