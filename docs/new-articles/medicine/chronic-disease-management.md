When grid-down conditions cut off pharmacies, people with chronic conditions face life-threatening medication gaps. This article covers the most common chronic diseases and how to manage them when medications run short or run out.

## Diabetes

Type 1 diabetes without insulin is fatal within days to weeks. There is no substitute. Insulin stockpiling and cold storage are the highest priority for anyone with Type 1 in your group.

Insulin storage without refrigeration: insulin lasts 28-30 days at room temperature (below 86°F/30°C). Above that, it degrades rapidly. Store in a clay pot evaporative cooler (zeer pot), underground cache, or spring-fed creek in a sealed waterproof container.

Type 2 diabetes without medication is manageable with diet:

1. Eliminate all sugar and refined carbohydrates immediately.
2. Focus on protein, fats, and non-starchy vegetables. Meat, fish, eggs, nuts, greens.
3. Eat small frequent meals rather than large ones. Prevents blood sugar spikes.
4. Exercise after eating — even a 15-minute walk reduces blood sugar significantly.
5. Cinnamon (1/2 teaspoon daily in food) has modest blood-sugar-lowering effects. Not a substitute for medication, but helps at the margins.
6. Monitor for signs of dangerously high blood sugar: excessive thirst, frequent urination, blurred vision, fruity breath odor, confusion. Fruity breath and confusion = diabetic emergency.

> Metformin is the most important Type 2 diabetes medication to stockpile. Fish antibiotics vendors do not sell it. Talk to your doctor about a 90-day supply rotation.

## Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

Without medication, focus on reducing blood pressure through behavior:

1. Reduce salt intake dramatically. Eliminate canned food, jerky, and salty preserved food if possible.
2. Increase potassium: bananas, potatoes, beans, greens, squash, fish.
3. Daily vigorous exercise — 30 minutes minimum.
4. Hibiscus tea: 3 cups daily has documented modest blood pressure reduction (8-10 mmHg systolic).
5. Garlic: 2-4 fresh cloves daily provides small reduction.
6. Stress management: deep breathing exercises, adequate sleep.
7. Reduce alcohol to zero.

Signs of hypertensive crisis (dangerously high): severe headache, chest pain, vision changes, nosebleed, confusion, shortness of breath. Have the person rest, sit upright, breathe slowly. This can cause stroke or heart attack.

## Asthma

Without inhalers, asthma attacks can be fatal. Stockpile inhalers and know their shelf life (albuterol remains effective 1-2 years past expiration, though potency decreases).

Managing without inhalers:

1. Avoid triggers: smoke (cook downwind, use Dakota fire holes), dust, cold air (breathe through a cloth), exertion.
2. Strong coffee or tea — caffeine is a mild bronchodilator. 2-3 cups of strong black coffee can provide modest relief during a mild attack.
3. Steam inhalation: breathe steam from boiling water with a towel over the head. Add eucalyptus or mint leaves if available.
4. Pursed-lip breathing: inhale through nose for 2 counts, exhale through pursed lips for 4 counts. Keeps airways open longer.
5. Stay upright — never lie flat during an attack. Lean forward with hands on knees.
6. Epinephrine (EpiPen) can reverse a severe asthma attack as a last resort. Same dose as anaphylaxis: 0.3mg IM adult.

## Thyroid Conditions

Hypothyroidism without levothyroxine: gradual decline over weeks. Increasing fatigue, cold sensitivity, constipation, depression, weight gain. Not immediately life-threatening but severely reduces function.

Hyperthyroidism without methimazole: more dangerous. Rapid heart rate, anxiety, weight loss, heat intolerance. Severe cases cause thyroid storm — fever, racing heart, confusion. This is a medical emergency.

## General Medication Stockpiling Principles

- Rotate stock: use the oldest medications, replace with new prescriptions.
- Most pills remain effective 1-5 years past expiration (FDA studies show this). Liquids and injectables degrade faster.
- Store medications in cool, dark, dry conditions. Heat and humidity destroy potency.
- Ask your doctor for 90-day prescriptions and refill early when insurance allows.
- Fish antibiotics (amoxicillin, ciprofloxacin, etc.) are the same chemical compounds as human versions. Cardiac, diabetic, thyroid, and psychiatric medications have no such over-the-counter equivalents.
